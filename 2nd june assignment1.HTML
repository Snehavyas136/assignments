My Favorite Hobby: Photography

Introduction

Photography is more than just clicking a button — it's a form of creative expression that captures moments in time. I developed an interest in photography when I was a teenager, and it has since become a passionate hobby of mine.

Whether it's a scenic sunset, a bustling city street, or the quiet look in someone's eyes, I find immense joy in freezing real-life moments and sharing them with others.

Why I Love Photography

One of the main reasons I love photography is the sense of freedom and discovery it brings. When I have my camera in hand, I'm constantly looking for beauty in the ordinary. It has helped me to appreciate the small details around me, like the texture of a weathered wall or the symmetry in architectural lines.

Conclusion

In conclusion, photography enriches my life by allowing me to explore both the world and myself. It's a creative outlet, a personal challenge, and a source of joy all rolled into one.

Early Life

Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire. From a young age, he showed an interest in mathematics and science. He was not always a top student, but his curiosity and deep thinking set him apart.

Einstein moved with his family to Italy and then later to Switzerland, where he studied at the Polytechnic Institute in Zurich. His love for physics and questioning of traditional ideas began early and stayed with him throughout his life.
